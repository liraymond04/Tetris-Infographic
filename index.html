What is Tetris?

Tetris is a puzzle game which is naturally very addictive, it can be played by a wide range of demographics and can be enjoyed by anyone. This is why it appeals to so many people around the world, and why it became so successful.

1984

Pajitnov developed the first playable version of Tetris for the Electronika 60 on June 6th while working at the Dorodnitsyn Computing Centre of the Soviet Academy of Sciences in Moscow, and later with the help of Vadin Gerasimov, ported the game to the IBM PC.

1987

Spectrum Holobyte would design and release a version Tetris for the IBM PC in the United States, making it the first game to be exported to the US from the Soviet Union.

1988

Many other companies would release their own versions of Tetris for different systems, but Pajitnov would still be unable to publish his own version, and would be forced to hand over the rights of the game to the Soviet government for 10 years.

1988

The Soviet organization in charge of Tetris, ELORG, signs rights to arcade versions of Tetris to Atari Games, and rights to home console versions of Tetris to Nintendo. Tengen, a console software division of Atari Games, releases a home console version of Tetris and loses a court case with Nintendo regarding their violation of their agreements with ELORG, resulting in Nintendo receiving rights to all versions of Tetris for consoles.

1989

Nintendo releases Tetris for the Nintendo Entertainment System, which sells 8 million copies worldwide. This would bethe first official console release of Tetris developed and published by Nintendo.

1996

The rights for Tetris are transferred from the USSR to Pajitnov. Pajitnov founds the Tetris Company, tightening down on Tetris products and Tetris-like games. Tetris has become a victim of universalization, as the Tetris company continues to take down Tetris-like games from the App store and Google Play store. Many developers of these games claim the company is limiting the diversity and creativity of the genre by removing games with any semblence to Tetris.

2018

Enhance games publishes a virtual reality version of Tetris developed by Japanese studios, Monstars and Resonair. The game brings a new experience for Tetris in a virtual world with amazing visuals that capture the player in the game world.

2019

Nintendo releases Tetris 99, a new take on the classic game. This new version features a new gamemode with 99 players, based on the trendy and popular video game genre, battle royale. Nintendo successfully incorporates the retro game with aspects from today's popular culture, creating a hybrid of puzzle game and a competitive free for all.
